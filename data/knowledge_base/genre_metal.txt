Metal is a high-intensity genre defined by distorted electric guitars, aggressive rhythms, and powerful vocal delivery. From heavy metal and thrash to doom and death metal, the genre spans wide stylistic territory unified by an emphasis on raw power and sonic extremity. Tempos are among the highest in popular music: thrash and death metal commonly exceed 160–200 BPM, while doom metal may slow to 40–60 BPM for oppressive heaviness.

Energy values are the highest of any genre, consistently 0.85–1.0 in most subgenres. Acousticness is extremely low (0.01–0.10) given the electric and electronic nature of metal production. Danceability is low to moderate (0.25–0.50) — metal encourages moshing and headbanging rather than structured dancing. Valence tends low (0.15–0.40), reflecting metal's affinity for darkness, aggression, and themes of conflict.

Characteristic instruments include heavily distorted electric guitars (often tuned down for maximum heaviness), bass guitar providing low-end weight, double-bass kick drum patterns for speed, and vocals ranging from melodic singing to death growls. Guitar techniques include palm muting, tremolo picking, and sweep arpeggios. Production is characterized by thick, layered guitar tones and powerful drum sounds.

Common moods include aggressive, intense, dark, and powerful. Listener use cases include extreme workouts, cathartic emotional release, competitive gaming, and the deeply communal experience of live metal concerts. Research suggests metal can serve as a healthy outlet for processing anger and frustration. It suits listeners who seek sonic extremity and emotional intensity, and who find beauty in controlled aggression and technical musicianship at its most demanding.
